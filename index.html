Quote 1

"But the big things--how we think, what we value--those you must choose for yourself. You can’t let anyone--or any society--determine those for you.”

Analysis

In today’s society, peer pressure has become increasingly prevalent due to the rise of social media. The opinions and lifestyles of peers are constantly being shared online which pressures people to adopt the beliefs of others. Sometimes, I change my behavior depending on what environment I’m in and attempt to fit in with the crowd. I hope that later in the future I will be able to act the same around everyone and not follow the collective opinion of others.

Quote 2

“‘It’s not just other people we need to forgive, Mitch,’ he finally whispered, ‘we also have to forgive ourselves.’”

Analysis

Often we are the harshest to oursevles and this self-criticism can be very harmful to personal growth . I regret doing many of the things that I’ve done and also regret not things that I chose not to do. I need to work on letting go of what has already happened and focus on living in the present rather than the past. Despite this, I think it’s important that I don’t completely neglect mistakes made previously to prevent making the same mistakes in the future.

Quote 3

“I can’t take care of the bank account, I can’t take out the garbage. But I can sit here with my dwindling days and look at what I think is important in life."

Analysis

Whenever I’ve faced a challenge that affected something important to me, I’ve always been frustrated over what happened or disappointed at what I did. This mindset is not helpful for personal growth and usually causes unnecessary stress. Instead, accepting the circumstances of my situation will allow me to focus on factors that I can control and help make the best of what I have.

Quote 4

“Mitch, I don’t allow myself any more self-pity than that. A little each morning, a few tears, and that’s all.”

Analysis

I’m guilty of feeling sorry for myself or feeling hopeless after facing significant failures/setbacks. Usually, I’ll develop a more pessimistic view of life and become much less productive for a period of time before I eventually get over it. Putting a limit on my self-pity can help me develop a mindset where I’m searching for solutions rather than obsessing over problems. I hope that in the future, I will be able to get rid of the negative mindset and focus on overcoming obstacles rather than being stuck on negative emotions.

Quote 5

“The culture doesn’t encourage you to think about such things until you’re about to die. We're so wrapped up with egotistical things, career, family, having enough money, meeting the mortgage, getting a new car, fixing the radiator when it breaks--we’re involved in trillions of little acts just to keep going. So we don’t get into the habit of standing back and looking at our lives and saying, Is this all?”

Analysis

I’ve been getting stuck into a loop of waiting to accomplish a milestone, and then immediately making another goal again. In middle school, I thought high school would be something amazing. However, it just turned out lackluster and I don’t have many memories from this current school year. Getting into the cycle of saying “My life will get better after I finish _________” is unhealthy because we’re always rushing to get to the next task instead of appreciating what we already have.

Quote 6

“Mitch, if you’re trying to show off for people at the top, forget it. They will look down at you anyhow. And if you’re trying to show off for people at the bottom, forget it. They will only envy you.”

Analysis

I think that validation from other people is something that has become increasingly important in our society. We are always trying to get approval from someone and it’s unhealthy because we’re changing ourselves to impress others. I’ve compared myself with others and tried fitting in with the crowd multiple times but it usually feels wrong and unauthentic. Seeking validation from others can easily lead to frustration and disappointment because they have different circumstances from you. Therefore, I am focusing on being true to both myself and my values.

Quote 7

“Forget what the culture says. I have ignored the culture much of my life. I am not going to be ashamed. What’s the big deal?”

Analysis

I’ve always tried to fit in with the rest of the crowd and I’ve never really questioned the opinions of the majority. Most of the time, I’ve assumed that the beliefs of the community apply to everybody and I’ve been blindly following societal norms. However, I think it’s important that I stop listening to societal expectations and focus on my own personal interests. This will allow me to create a path that is aligned with my values and goals.

Quote 8

“Most of us all walk around as if we’re sleepwalking. We really don’t experience the world fully, because we’re half-asleep, doing things we automatically think we have to do.”

Analysis

I’ve gotten into a routine in which I have the same tasks and obligations on the same days of the week. As a result, the year has flown by and I have little meaningful memories from both school and my personal life. I have not been reflecting on my choices and have instead been stuck in the cycle of doing the same things over and over. By ignoring mundane tasks and instead reflecting on my priorities, I think I will be able to redirect my efforts towards that aligns with my goals and values.
